Witness Statements - Office Staff Interviews

Employee Witness Interviews

Document Type: Formal Witness Statements | Conducted By: Security Department | Date: January 15-16, 2024
Rachel Torres
Receptionist, Front Desk
Interview Date: January 15, 2024 - 6:45 PM

Q: What did you observe on January 15th that might be relevant?

"I saw Sarah Miller leave the building around 2:15 PM. She told me she was going to grab lunch, but I noticed she came back about 45 minutes later carrying the same purse with no food or coffee. I remember thinking it was odd since she usually brings lunch from home."

Q: Did you see anyone else unusual that afternoon?

"Marcus from IT walked by around 2:40 PM carrying his toolkit. He was heading toward the elevators. He seemed like he was in a hurry, which isn't normal for him - he's usually very relaxed."
Jennifer Kim
Executive Assistant to CFO Sarah Miller
Interview Date: January 16, 2024 - 9:00 AM

Q: Can you describe Sarah Miller's activities on January 15th?

"Sarah asked me to hold all her calls from 2:00 PM to 3:30 PM because she said she needed to focus on the quarterly reports. But when I brought her coffee around 2:20 PM, she wasn't in her office. Her computer was on, and there was a document open, but she wasn't there."

Q: When did she return?

"She came back around 3:00 PM. When I asked if she wanted the coffee reheated, she seemed flustered and said she'd just stepped out to use the restroom. But that didn't make sense - she was gone for at least 40 minutes, and the restroom is right next to her office."

Q: Did Sarah mention David Park recently?

"Yes, she's been very stressed about his project budget. Last week she said something like, 'If David's project fails, it could actually save the company money in the short term.' I found that comment strange."
Tom Bradford
Junior Software Developer, sits near David's office
Interview Date: January 15, 2024 - 7:15 PM

Q: Did you notice anything unusual near David's office today?

"I saw Lisa Chen walking in the hallway outside David's office around 2:35 PM. She was on her phone and looked like she was waiting for something. I thought maybe she had a meeting with David, but she didn't knock on his door."

Q: How long was she there?

"Maybe 5-10 minutes. Then Marcus from IT showed up with his access card and opened David's door. Lisa walked away right after Marcus went inside. I didn't think much of it at the time."

Q: Did you see Marcus leave?

"Yeah, about 5-7 minutes later. He was carrying his toolkit, walking pretty fast. He didn't look at anyone - just went straight to the elevator."
Building Security Guard - James Chen
Security Officer, Main Entrance
Interview Date: January 15, 2024 - 8:00 PM

Q: Do you monitor who enters and exits the building?

"Yes, all employees badge in and out. I also visually check larger bags or packages. On January 15th at approximately 2:55 PM, Marcus Webb left the building carrying his large IT toolkit - the metal case he uses for computer repairs."

Q: Is that unusual?

"Not particularly - he often takes equipment home or to other offices. But I do remember he seemed nervous. He was sweating even though it was cool outside, and he kept looking back at the elevators. When I asked him if everything was okay, he said he was just running late for an appointment."

Q: Did you see him return?

"Yes, he came back about 20 minutes later at 3:15 PM, but without the toolkit. He said he'd dropped it off at his car for repairs later. That struck me as odd because he usually keeps it at his desk."
