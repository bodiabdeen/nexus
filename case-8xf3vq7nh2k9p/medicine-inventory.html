Introduction

The Blackwood Estate maintains a locked medicine cabinet containing various medicinal compounds, tonics, and substances for family use. Dr. Helena Ashworth, as family physician since 1883, has been responsible for maintaining inventory and preparing medications. She holds one of three keys to the cabinet (the others held by Lord Blackwood and estate manager James Pembroke).

The following inventory represents the contents of the medicine cabinet as of October 31st, 1889, along with access logs maintained by Dr. Ashworth showing all entries to the cabinet during October 1889.

Arsenic Inventory Discrepancy

Significant discrepancy detected in arsenic inventory. Analysis indicates deliberate manipulation of records.
Date Expected Quantity Actual Quantity Discrepancy
September 1st, 1889 100 grams 100 grams (verified) None
September 15th, 1889 100 grams 95 grams (per log) 5 grams - recorded as "tonic preparation"
October 1st, 1889 95 grams 90 grams (per log) 5 grams - recorded as "rat poison estate use"
October 15th, 1889 90 grams 85 grams (per log) 5 grams - recorded as "medicinal tonic"
October 29th, 1889 85 grams 62 grams (actual count Nov 1st) 23 grams - NOT RECORDED IN LOG
23 grams of arsenic trioxide went missing between October 15th and October 29th with NO corresponding log entry. This represents more than enough for a lethal dose (70-180mg total lethal). The missing quantity could produce approximately 150-200 lethal doses.
Analysis Note: The initial small withdrawals (5 grams each) were properly logged and match the pattern of chronic low-level poisoning detected in the autopsy. The final large withdrawal of 23 grams was NOT logged, suggesting intentional concealment of the theft.
